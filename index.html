    What's Clio&Erato

    The Clio&Erato magazine takes its name from two Greek muses. In ancient Greek religion and mythology, the Muses are the inspirational goddesses of literature, science, and the arts. They were considered the source of the knowledge embodied in the poetry, lyric songs, and myths. Clio is the muse of history and Erato is the muse of lyric poetry, in order to represent the magazine's central theme: Women's poetry across history.

    Three issues have been chosen to represent poetry, in a chronological order, through centuries: the Ancient Era, the XIX century and the XXI century.

    A famous poetess is proposed for each issue: Sappho represents the Ancient Era; Emily Dickinson represents the XIX century; and Rupi Kaur represents the XXI century. You can now read three poems of each poetess.
